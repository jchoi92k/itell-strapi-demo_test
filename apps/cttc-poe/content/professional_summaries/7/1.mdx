This section of the Principles of Macroeconomics discusses the recent phenomenon of economic growth, starting from the Industrial Revolution. It explains how economic growth was influenced by factors like slavery-based societies, global trading routes, and standardized trading institutions. The text also highlights the importance of institutional changes, such as the rule of law and property rights, in fostering economic growth. It further discusses the disparity in economic growth among nations, with some countries experiencing rapid growth while others remain at subsistence levels. The text ends by emphasizing the role of a country's legal system in protecting property and contractual rights for economic growth.
